Uber promises investigation after former engineer blogs about rampant sexual harassment

By Lynda O'Connor

Susan Fowler published a nearly 3,000-word blog post last Sunday describing a nightmarish workplace culture in which male superiors solicited her for sex and human resources officers shrugged off her concerns about sexist company. Arianna Huffington, board member of Uber, is spearheading an investigation into the allegations and potential policy changes.

Google confirms its acquisition of data science community Kaggle

By Matthew Lynley
Google today said it is acquiring Kaggle, an online service that hosts data science and machine learning competitions. Google made the announcement at its Google Cloud Next conference on March 7 in San Francisco. This acquisition would allow Google the immediate ability to broaden its influence in the AI community. Recently, Google has been facing more competition in the AI space as other competitors are mastering autonomous driving and deep learning. Purchasing Kaggle, and its mindshare community, allow Google to improve recruiting efforts with candidates specializing in deep learning like Pinterest.

Hinge is testing a personal assistant service called Audrey to help you get more dates

By Sarah Perez (@sarahintampa)
Dating app Hinge just launched a twist on matching that moves away from swiping to “stories,” in order to help people more long-terms relationships. This focus on authenticity has led to the company to test a new project called Audrey. Audrey is a personal assistant that messages your matches for you and schedules dates. It can take time to find a perfect match, filter messages, and begin introductory chats with potential partners. It also takes times to determine based on your needs, matches and who's interested in going out versus just needing more friends. Audrey streamlines all of these interactions. Fitbit's next goal: putting you to sleep

By Lisa Eadicicco
Fitbit, the former leader of the wearable space, is now focusing on making fitness tracking easier. In order to remain competitive, Fitbit is launching new product line to improve users' experience with health monitoring by incorporating sleep metrics. The Alta HR wristband is a slim fitness wearable that include a heart rate monitor, longer battery lif, and sleep tracking features known as Sleep Stage. The metrics will include the frequency of awake time, and time slept and awakened. This new product announcement comes at the end of dismal earnings in February. Alta HR will hit retail stores for $149.95 in April.More info here: Fitbit's Alta HR
